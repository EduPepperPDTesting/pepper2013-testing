Graphing Quadratic Equations

Graphs of y = ax2

It is understood that a cannot be zero. Hence, there are only two possibilities: a > 0 and a < 0. Because a represents unlimited numerical values, the equation y = ax2 represents a different graph for each numerical value.

Case 2: a < 0

The shape is the same as in Case 1. The only difference is that the graph of y = ax2 opens downward when a < 0, whereas the graph of y = ax2 opens upward when a > 0. The two graphs are symmetrical with respect to the x-axis.
